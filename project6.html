Problem Statement

  • Viewfinder is a new start-up dedicated to designing, selling and celebrating the fusion of graphics and fashion. Also, Viewfinder has a goal to work as an Online community/market that brings creative artists and those who admire their work altogether. Viewfinder expects to reach $6 M in the first year, and $10 M the next, which means Viewfinder aims to be recognized as one of the number one Online suppliers of personalized apparel with excellent customer support service.

    E-commerce Trends in 2020
    E-commerce is a rapidly growing market and new trends constantly arise. To be able to run a successful e-commerce business an online seller should be ready to adapt to them. Fashion consumers will also have more buying power, as the number of potential customers is projected to grow to more than 1.2 billion by 2020. The good news for fashion is that the majority of these new consumers are within the 16 to 24 and 25 to 34 age groups. New generations of young consumers are entering the market, their shop- ping requirements, fears, and desires are significantly different from those that older shoppers have. Analysts forecast that mobile e-commerce will reach $ 284 billion, or 45% of the total U.S. eCommerce market, by 2020.

    The growing importance of mobile
    Females are marginally more likely to only use a smartphone, 12.4% compared to 11.6%, but this varies by age. More young males are mobile-only, 18.5% for those aged 21-24 compared to 16.2% for females. However, females lead among older generations, 5.8% for those 65+ compared to 4.1%. This comes as mobile internet speeds become faster and people spend longer on mobile devic- es each day. Advertising spend has followed, with a mobile set to account for 95% of all internet ad growth this year. The channel continues to be boosted by the potential for personalization.
    The growing importance of Social media
    Online time in North America has stabilised in recent years, according to the latest data from GlobalWebIn- dex. While daily average Online consumption reached a new high in H1 2018 at 6:27 (hours:minutes), this is still only five and two minutes greater than the levels seen in 2013 and 2014. Except for a more significant drop in 2015, online time has remained between 6:19 and 6:27 in the last six years.
